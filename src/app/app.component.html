PERIODIC TABLE

A periodic table is a tabular arrangement of chemical elements, organized by their atomic number, electron configuration, and recurring chemical properties. It provides a systematic way to represent the relationships between elements based on their fundamental properties, such as atomic mass and electron configuration. The periodic table is a cornerstone of chemistry and is used to predict the behavior of elements and their compounds, as well as to guide research and exploration in the field of chemistry.
